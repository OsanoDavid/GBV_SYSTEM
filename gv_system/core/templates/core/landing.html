Recognizing Online Violence

Technology-facilitated gender-based violence manifests in various forms. Select a category during reporting to help the triage team react swiftly.

Click any category below to expand and read more about each type of online violence.

Cyberstalking happens when someone repeatedly follows, monitors, or harasses you online. This includes continual messages, fake accounts, location tracking, photo tagging without consent, or constant monitoring of your social activity.
Doxing or intimate leaks is when someone shares your private details or images without permission. This includes posting your address, phone number, workplace details, or private photos to shame, threaten, or pressure you.
Impersonation is when someone pretends to be you online. This includes cloned accounts, fake profiles, or messages sent in your name to damage your reputation, steal money, or deceive your contacts.
Threats and hate speech include messages that insult, threaten, or encourage violence because of your gender, religion, race, or identity. It also covers public campaigns meant to humiliate, intimidate, or pressure you to stay silent.
